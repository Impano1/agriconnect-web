Farming Techniques

Crop Rotation

Crop rotation involves planting different crops in the same area over a sequence of growing seasons. It helps improve soil health, reduce pests and diseases, and increase crop yield.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is an environmentally sensitive approach to pest management that aims to minimize risks to human health, beneficial insects, and the environment. It involves a combination of techniques such as biological control, crop rotation, and the use of resistant varieties.

Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation is a method of watering plants by applying water directly to the root zone through a network of tubes and emitters. It conserves water, reduces weed growth, and delivers nutrients more efficiently to plants.

No-Till Farming

No-till farming is a conservation agriculture practice that eliminates plowing and minimizes soil disturbance. It helps improve soil structure, increase water retention, and reduce erosion.

Cover Cropping

Cover cropping involves planting specific crops during fallow periods to protect and improve soil health. It helps prevent erosion, suppress weeds, and enhance soil fertility.

Precision Agriculture

Precision agriculture utilizes technology such as GPS, sensors, and drones to optimize crop production practices. It enables farmers to make data-driven decisions, reduce inputs, and maximize yields.
